Animator and illustrator living and working in the Netherlands.

My personal projects strive to express a slightly more magical but still recognizable world. They are guided by my philosophical background as well as by the imagination, which can sometimes be at odds with each other.

I work with a hybrid of analog and digital tools, but will always strive to keep most of my drawings rooted in actual, tactile, physical reality. There is a certain relief in a drawing not disappearing once the computer shuts down.

This website serves as a showcase for my personal work, accompanied by a blog detailing both the technical and creative process behind various animations and drawings. I try to implement as many open source tools as possible, underpinning my belief that filmmaking should be accessible and open to anyone.

Here is a list of open source (filmmaking) tools I have found to be very useful over the years:

  • Blender (Fully featured 3D (and 2D) kit)
  • Opentoonz (Studio Ghibli compositor geared towards handdrawn animation)
  • GTS (Studio Ghibli scanning tool for handdrawn frames)
  • Audacity (Simple but effective audio editing)
  • DJV (Videoplayer with all the right tools for reviewing clips)
  • Krita (Digital draw/paint software with excellent 2D animation environment)
  • FFMPEG (Fully featured video/audio conversion)
