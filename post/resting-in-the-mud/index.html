Resting in the Mud

September 18, 2021

Another attempt at ‘mixed media’ animation, combining analog charcoal drawings with digitally animated characters.

There is one shot in the film A Hidden Life (2019) that keeps popping up in my mind. What fascinates me about it is an animal many times larger optically than the human in the same composition, due to the wide-angle lenses used by cinematographer Jörg Widmer. The shot doesn’t even last more than two seconds, but it does leave a lasting impression (as does the whole movie by the way).

I decided to create an animation based on this style of composition. The characters and scenery were adopted, but the animation itself is not based on the actual movement in the film.

The fore- and background I made as seperate charcoal drawings, to be later composited behind and in front of the characters.

The characters I then animated and colored on seperate layers in Krita:
